The same case. Two timelines.

The hallucinations were not the final problem. The replacement citation was.

Mata v. Avianca made the risk visible: six fake citations reached a federal judge. But the deeper problem appears after the fake citations are removed, when a real authority still creates adversarial exposure.

What happened

Mata v. Avianca reaches the court with six fabricated citations

  1. ChatGPT generates six fabricated authorities.

    Varghese v. China Southern, Shaboon v. Egyptair, Petersen v. Iran Air, Martinez v. Delta, Estate of Durden v. KLM, and Miller v. United Airlines all appear plausible in context.

  2. The citations are “verified” by the same system that invented them.

    They are accepted as real and filed in federal court.

  3. Opposing counsel cannot locate the cases.

    The court is alerted, the filing becomes suspect, and the issue shifts from argument quality to attorney credibility.

  4. The result is sanctions and reputational damage.

    The visible failure is obvious — but only after filing.

Punchline: the fake citations were caught too late, after the filing had already become a liability.
With Citation Defender

The fake citations are removed — and the real risk is still found

  1. Level 1 flags all six fabricated citations before filing.

    The obvious failure never reaches the court.

  2. A real replacement citation is identified.

    Piper Aircraft Co. v. Reyno, 454 U.S. 235 (1981) appears to help: it is real, quotable, and relevant to forum non conveniens.

  3. Level 2 shows the fit is narrower than the draft suggests.

    The case supports deference to plaintiff’s forum choice, but not as broadly as the brief would like.

  4. Level 3 shows the replacement citation is still unsafe.

    The same opinion also says deference is reduced when the plaintiff is foreign. Mata is a Colombian citizen, so opposing counsel can use the same authority to argue that the Southern District of New York should decline jurisdiction.

  5. The brief is adjusted before filing.

    The attorney narrows the use of Piper, prepares the rebuttal, and leads with stronger authority that does not cut both ways.

Punchline: Level 3 reveals when a citation is real, accurate, and still hands ammunition to the other side.

Most tools stop after replacing the fake citation. Citation Defender continues until it can answer the harder question: is the real replacement actually safe to use?
